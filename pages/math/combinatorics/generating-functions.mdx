# Generating Functions
_This note presents an overview of generating functions._

~question~. Let ${A = \set{2,3,6,7}}$ and let ${B=\set{3,4,5,8}.}$ How many ways can we sum to 10 by using one element from ${A}$ and one element from ${B?}$ We can answer this with brute-force:

$$
	\ax{
		~ & 3 & 4 & 5 & 8 \\
		2 & 2+3\eq5\no{0} & 2+4\eq6\no{0} & 2+5\eq7\no{0} & 2+8\eq10 \\
		3 & 3+3\eq6\no{0} & 3+4\eq5\no{0} & 3+5\eq8\no{0} & 3+8\eq11 \\
		6 & 6+3\eq9\no{0} & 6+4\eq10 & 6+5\eq11 & 6+8\eq14 \\
		7 & 7+3\eq10 & 7+4\eq11 & 7+5\eq12 & 7+8\eq13 \\
	}
$$

Examining the cases, we see that there are three ways: ${\set{\px{2,8}~ \px{6,4}~ \px{7,3}}.}$
