## Number Theory

**First Corollary of Associativity.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ^+.}$

$$
	a + b = 0 \implies \begin{cases}
	\begin{aligned}
		b &= -a \\
		a &= -b
	\end{aligned}
	\end{cases}
$$

**Second Corollary of Associativity.** Let ${a \in \ZZ^+.}$

$$
	a = -(-a) = 0
$$

**Third Corollary of Associativity.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ^+.}$ Then:

$$
\begin{aligned}
	-(a+b) = -a + (-b) \\
	-(a+b) = -a - b
\end{aligned}
$$

**First Law of Closure.**

$$
	(a,b \in Z^+) \implies (a + b \in Z^+)
$$

**Second Law of Closure.**

$$
	(a,b \in Z^-) \implies (a + b \in \ZZ^-)
$$

**First Law of Closure, Corollary 1.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ^+.}$

$$
	a + b = c \implies \begin{cases}
		\begin{aligned}
			a &= c - b \\
			b &= c - a
		\end{aligned}
	\end{cases}
$$

**Cancellation Law of Addition.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ^+.}$ Then:

$$
	(a + b = a + c) \implies (b = c)
$$

**Zero Addition Law.** Let ${a \in \ZZ^+.}$ Then:

$$
	(a + b = a)  \implies (b = 0)
$$

**Commutative Law of Multiplication.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	ab = ba
$$

**Associative Law of Multiplication.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(ab)c = a(bc)
$$

**Identity Law of Multiplication.** Let ${a \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	1a = a
$$

**Zero Multiplication Law.** Let ${a \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	0a = 0
$$

**Distributive Law 1.** Let ${a,b,c \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	a(b+c) = ab + ac
$$

**Distributive Law 2.** Let ${a,b,c \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(b+c)a = ba + ca
$$

**Negativity Law of Multiplication.** Let ${a \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(-1)a = -a
$$

**General Negativity Law of Multiplication.** Let ${a \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	-(ab) = (-a)b = a(-b)
$$

**Negativity Law of Multiplication: Corollary 1.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ.}$
Then:

$$
	(-a)(-b) = ab
$$

**Definition of a Power.** Let ${m,n \in \ZZ^+.}$ Then:

$$
	a^{m+n} = a^m a^n
$$

**Products of a Power Law.** Let ${m,n \in \ZZ^+.}$ Then:

$$
	(a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}
$$

**Distributions of a Power Law.** Let ${m,n \in \ZZ^+.}$ Then:

$$
	(ab)^n = a^n b^n
$$

**Expansion 1.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2
$$

**Expansion 2.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2
$$

**Expansion 3.** Let ${a,b \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a + b)(a - b) = a^2 - b^2
$$

### Parity

**Definition: Even Number.** Let ${a,k \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a = 2k) \implies a \in \bb{E}
$$

where ${\bb{E}}$ is the set of all even integers.

**Definition: Odd Number.** Let ${a,k \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a = 2k + 1) \implies (a \in \bb{O})
$$

where ${\bb{O}}$ is the set of all odd integers.

**Sum of Evens Theorem.** The sum of evens is an even.

$$
	(a,b \in \bb{E}) \implies (a + b \in \bb{E})
$$

**Sum of Even & Odd Theorem.** The sum of an even and an odd is an odd.

$$
	(a \in \bb{E}) \land (b \in \bb{O}) \implies (a + b \in \bb{O})
$$

**Sum of Odd & Odd Theorem.** The sum of an odd and an odd is an even.

$$
	(a \in \bb{O}) \land (b \in \bb{O}) \implies (a + b \in \bb{E})
$$

**Product of Even Rule.** The product of an even and any positive integer
is an even.

$$
	(a \in \bb{E}) \land (b \in \ZZ^+) \implies (ab \in \bb{E})
$$

**Square Parity Rule.** Let ${a \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	\begin{aligned}
		a \in \bb{E} &\iff a^2 \in \bb{E} \\
		a \in \bb{O} &\iff a^2 \in \bb{O}
	\end{aligned}
$$

I.e.:

- If ${a^2}$ is even, ${a}$ is even.
- If ${a}$ is even, ${a^2}$ is even.
- If ${a^2}$ is odd, ${a}$ is odd.
- If ${a}$ is odd, ${a^2}$ is odd.

**Cube Parity Rule.** Let ${a \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	\begin{aligned}
		a \in \bb{E} \iff a^3 \in \bb{E} \\
		a \in \bb{O} \iff a^3 \in \bb{O} \\
	\end{aligned}
$$

I.e.:

- If ${a^3}$ is even, ${a}$ is even.
- If ${a}$ is even, ${a^3}$ is even.
- If ${a^3}$ is odd, ${a}$ is odd.
- If ${a}$ is odd, ${a^3}$ is odd.

**Parity Rule of -1.** Let ${n \in \ZZ^+.}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
	n \in \bb{E} &\implies (-1)^n = 1 \\
	n \in \bb{O} &\implies (-1)^n = -1
\end{aligned}
$$

### Divisibility

**Definition: Divisibility.** Let ${d \in Z^+,}$ ${n \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	d \divs n \iff n = dk ~\tt{where}~ k \in \ZZ.
$$

**Divisibility Corollary 1.**

$$
	d \divs n \implies \begin{cases}
		\begin{aligned}
			&\tt{$d$ divides $n$} \\
			&\tt{$n$ is divisible by $d$} \\
			&\tt{$n$ divided by $d$ has remainder 0}
		\end{aligned}
	\end{cases}
$$

**Divisibility Corollary 2.** Let ${a \in \ZZ^+}$ and ${b,c \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a \divs b) \land (a \divs c) \implies (~~~a \divs (b+c)~~~)
$$

I.e., if ${a}$ divides ${b}$ and ${a}$ divides ${c,}$ then ${a}$ divides
${b+c.}$

**Divisibility Corollary 3.** Let ${a \in \ZZ^+}$ and ${b,c \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	(a \divs b) \implies (a \divs bc)
$$

I.e., if ${a}$ divides ${b,}$ then ${a}$ divides any multiple of ${b.}$

**Divisibility Chaining Rule.** Let ${a \in \ZZ^+}$ and ${b,c \in \ZZ.}$
Then:

$$
	(a \divs b) \land (b \divs c) \implies (a \divs c)
$$

I.e., if ${a}$ divides ${b}$ and ${b}$ divides ${c,}$ then ${a}$ divides
${c.}$

**General Divisibility Chaining Rule.** Let ${a \in \ZZ^+}$ and
${b,c \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
\begin{aligned}
	(a \divs b) \land (b \divs c) \implies (a \divs mb + nc) \\[1em]
	\tt{where} m,n \in \ZZ
\end{aligned}
$$

I.e., if ${a}$ divides ${b}$ and ${b}$ divides ${c,}$ then ${a}$ divides
the sum of multiples of ${b}$ and multiples of ${c.}$

#### Rational Numbers

**Definition: Rational Number.** Let ${m \in \ZZ,}$ and ${n \in \ZZ^+}$ Then:

$$
	\dfrac{m}{n} \in \bb{Q}
$$

where ${\bb{Q}}$ is the set of all rational numbers.

**Cross Multiplication Rule.** Let ${m,n,r,s \in \ZZ,}$ ${n \neq 0,}$ and
${s \neq 0.}$ Then:

$$
	\dfrac{m}{n} = \dfrac{r}{s} \iff ms = rn
$$

**Convention: Indistinction of ùëö/1.** Let ${m \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	\dfrac{m}{1} = m
$$

Therefore, ${\ZZ \subset \bb{Q}.}$ I.e., all integers are rationals, but not
all rationals are integers.

**Cancellation Rule of Rationals.** Let ${a,n \in \ZZ^{\pm}}$ and
${m \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	\dfrac{am}{an} = \dfrac{m}{n}
$$

**Cancellation Rule of Rationals: Corollary 1.** Let ${n \in \ZZ^+}$ and
${m \in \ZZ.}$ Then:

$$
	\dfrac{-m}{n} = \dfrac{m}{-n}
$$
